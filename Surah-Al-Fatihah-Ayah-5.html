Surah Al Fatihah Ayah 5

اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ

Guide us

  • The most important thing we need help in is guidance
  • Allah gives you lots of things without asking
  • Guidance Allah only gives you only if you ask Him
  • Allah guides whom He wants
  • Guidance is not yours to keep
  • We have to ask for guidance over and over again
  • Guidance cannot be passed down to your children
  • Guidance is so precious and so easily removed
  • Even the Messenger has to ask for it again and again
  • We cannot guide anyone
  • We can only share what we have in knowledge
  • We must be desperate in asking for guidance

The Comparison between Guidance and Water

  • If you are thirsty you will ask for water
  • If you are not dying of thirst then you will ask once and forget it even if you did not get any
  • But if you are dying of thirst then you will ask again and again
  • You will be more and more desperate in asking for water
  • How desperate you are is shown in your heart and attitude, not just your words

The Master and Slave Relationship

  • A slave always does what the master tells them
  • A slave never does what he or she wants
  • We cannot be slaves of Allah if we do not know what Allah told us to do
  • As soon as you accept Allah as Master and yourself as slave then we must ask for guidance
  • Allah mentions Master and Guidance together throughout the Quran
  • A slave must get guidance from the Master

Guidance and Gifts

  • When you are lost in the desert then the most important gift you can get is guidance
  • Because even if you have water but no guidance then you will eventually run out of water and die
  • But if you have guidance then you can be saved

Knowledge and Guidance

  • You can have a lot of knowledge of Quran but not have guidance
  • You can be a Muslim and not have guidance
  • Guidance is not one act
  • When someone takes the shahadah they took their first guided act
  • Every moment we make a series of decisions
  • We could make the right decision or the wrong decision
  • In every decision that we make we need Allah's Guidance and advice
  • Allah gives us constant guidance and advice throughout the Quran
  • Knowledge and Islam you can keep and increase
  • Guidance is only increased by Allah
  • The moment you stop appreciating Allah's Guidance it could be taken away from you
  • The practical gauge of how guided you are is how focused you are in your salah

The Straight Path

  • The straight path is a balance in this life
  • It is a long and wide road
  • There is no confusion on this road
  • The road is clear
  • Other people are on the road too
  • Some people are ahead of you, some people are behind, and some people are next to you
  • We ask directions to a destination and not a path
  • In the Fatihah we are asking Allah directions to the path
  • In this life our destination is the path
  • We must progress and become better and better each day
  • We should not compare ourselves to anyone else except ourselves
  • We could become inspired by each other, but we each have our own path
  • As you gain more knowledge, you should be more humble

The Straight Path that is upward

  • This path is straight and upwards
  • Guidance pulls you up and your sins pull you down
  • You are either going up or down but never stationary
  • If you fall from a short height you will be ok and get back up
  • If you fall from such a high height then when you fall, you sink into the earth
  • You then get so immersed in the duniya and it becomes difficult to get back up
