Surah Al Fatihah Ayah 3

مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ

Introduction

  • The first three ayat of the Fatihah is Allah introducing Himself to us
  • Praise and Thanks belongs to Allah because He is Master of People, Ar Rahman, and Ar Raheem
  • Praise and Thanks is for Allah because He is Malik Yom Ad Deen
  • They are all grammatically connected

The Master and The Slave Example

  • There is a Master and a slave
  • The Master tells the slave you are free to do anything you want, but just do not cross this fence
  • So the slave is hanging around the fence then trips and falls on the other side
  • He immediately gets up and the Master sees him and says nothing
  • The next day the slave pretends to fall on the other side of the fence
  • The Master says nothing
  • The next day slave has one foot on one side of he fence and another foot on the other side of the fence
  • The Master says nothing
  • Years go by and the slave spends all his time on the other side of the fence
  • The Master says nothing
  • Then one day the Master calls the slave and says remember when I told you not to cross the fence
  • The slave says yes I remember
  • The Master says I recorded all the times you crossed the fence and I decided to punish now all at once

Reality

  • Allah is our Master
  • Allah told us this is halal and this is haram
  • Human beings accidently slip and fall into sin
  • We immediately repent and turn back to Allah
  • Then we realize nothing happened and Allah is Ar Rahman and Ar Raheem
  • We fall more into sin
  • When people advise us to stop sinning, we say its ok Allah is extremely loving and caring

Maalik and Malik

  • Maalik means Master or Owner
  • An owner owns small things but not big things
  • An owner can control every small detail
  • Malik means king
  • A king owns the entire kingdom
  • The king has no control over every small detail
  • Allah is the Owner and King of the day of judgment
  • On the day of judgment big things will happen, such as the oceans will boil and the mountains will crumble
  • Every small deed of the human being will also be counted on the day of Judgment
  • Allah takes complete control of judgment day by combining the two names Maalik and Malik

Yom

  • Yom means day
  • A day is a unit of time
  • Human beings can own certain things, but we can never own time
  • Allah takes complete ownership of time
  • Allah can make a day long like 50,000 years and short like the blink of an eye
  • Time is like a bag and we are inside the bag
  • Allah owns time and everything inside time

Ad Deen

  • Ad Deen means The Judgment
  • It means to deal with someone or how you will be dealt with
  • Allah will deal with everyone and everything on this day
  • It is the completing of the record so we say Alhamdulillah
  • Humans beings desire justice especially for other people
  • This world is unfair and has no justice
  • People do good and a lot of bad is done to them
  • People do evil and they seem to get away with it
  • Justice will be served on Judgment day
  • Nothing you did will go uncounted for

Love and Care, Punishment, and Justice

  • Think of a number line
  • You have positive, neutral, and negative
  • Negative is the Punishment of Allah
  • Neutral is the Justice of Allah
  • Positive is the Love and Care of Allah
  • Allah does not compare His Love and Care with His Punishment
  • Allah compares His Love and Care with His Justice
  • Everything Allah does is postive and neutral, and not negative or any form of punishment
  • It is the Justice of Allah that leads people into the hellfire
  • It is the Love and Care of Allah that leads people into the paradise

Conclusion

  • Allah tells us My slave what has deluded you from Your Generous Master
  • You remembered Ar Rahman and Ar Rahmeen, but forgot about Malik Yom Ad Deen
  • How can you do that to yourself
