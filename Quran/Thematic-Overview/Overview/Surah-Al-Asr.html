Surah Al Asr

Introduction

  • Surah Al Asr is the summary of the entire Quran
  • This surah is the minumum Allah expects from us
  • This surah contains three ayat but one sentence

Allah takes an Oath

  • Allah swears by time
  • To get your attention
  • To prepare you for the subject of the oath
  • When Allah is angry
  • When Allah is not being believed
  • Swear is used as a testimony in court
  • To give you evidence
  • To describe a witness
  • Time is like an old man who is a witness to how mankind has been in loss
  • Time is the greatest witness that humans beings are in loss
  • Time is a witness to a lot of the same things

Human Beings are in loss

  • For sure each and every human being is drowning in loss
  • Time is like a sense of urgency that is always running out and being squeezed out of us
  • Time is the ultimate proof that we are in loss
  • We do not think about anyone else but yourself
  • We are truly ourselves when we are alone
  • We must first realize who we truly are and that we are in loss
  • Allah said we are in some loss and not total loss

The Four Parts of Surah Asr

  1. Except for those who believe
    • Imagine you are drowning and asleep
    • The first thing you need to do is wake and realize you are drowning in loss
    • The first thing to believe is that you are drowning in loss
    • It is a small group of people who believe and acquire the other four qualities
    • They believe in Allah, the angels, the books, the Messengers, the last day, destiny the good and bad
  2. Those who do good deeds
    • The next step is to do good actions that save you from drowning
    • Prove your belief with your actions
    • Deeds that fix your day
    • Just doing a few handful of good deeds
    • Deeds that are less than ten
    • Allah is not asking us for a lot
  3. Those who counsel each other truthfully
    • Then you realize you are tied to your relative
    • You must wake them up and help each other to survive
    • Counsel each other truthfully
    • This deen is not just about ourselves
    • We must take care of others
    • Find the right time and words to give counsel
    • Giving heart felt advice and soften their heart
    • You are giving advice and also willing to receive advice
    • Counseling truthfully and sincerly
    • It takes great courage and strength to speak the truth
  4. Those who counsel each other patiently
    • You must continue to preserve and encourage each other to keep going
    • Or else you both will drown in loss and die

Concluding Remarks

  • The last ayah of the surah is in the past tense
  • Our role models are found in the past
  • We take advice from people in the past
  • The advice in the Quran is different for different people
  • Having people in your life who can openly advise you towards truth and patience
