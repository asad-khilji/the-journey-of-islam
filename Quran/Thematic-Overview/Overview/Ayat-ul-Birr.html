Ayat ul Birr

Introduction

  • Surah Al Baqarah begins with talking about three groups of people believers, disbelievers, and hypocrites
  • Then it mentions the story of Adam and lessons on controlling our greed
  • Then it mentions the favors Allah bestowed upon the Children of Israel but they were ungrateful
  • Then Allah mentions the favors allah bestowed upon Ibrahim and he was grateful
  • Ibrahim had raised the foundations of the Kaaba with his son Ismael
  • Then Allah changed the direction of prayer from Jersalem to Makkah so the Prophet is happy
  • Ramadan is the inauguration month of a new nation for Muslims.
  • Previously Muslims had prayed in the same direction as the Jews, and fasted at the same times
  • With the revelation of the Quran, the Muslims now had their own book, their own capital (Makkah), and their own set of religious requirements
  • As part of this inauguration, Allah redefines what the definition of a good person is in Surah Al-Baqarah:177

Who is a Good Person?

  • The word goodness or birr is related to the word barr or land because goodness is like safety on land
  • Whereas sin is like an ocean in which you are drowning in loss
  • Also, land is firm, just as the definition of Goodness is firm
  • Even as our cultures and situations change, our definition of goodness remains fixed
  • Its important to note that this discussion is about the highest level of goodness
  • If a person doesn’t meet these requirements, it doesn’t mean that they don’t do anything good;
  • It just means that they haven’t reached the highest level of goodness as Allah defines it

What is required to be Goodness?

  1. To Believe in Allah
    • It is important to note that this is the first pre requiste to goodness
  2. To Believe in the Last Day and that you will be held accountable for your deeds
  3. To Believe in the Angels, Books, and Messengers
    • This is the order of revelation
    • The angels delivered the books to the Messengers
    • And the Messengers delivered the message to us
    • Without revelation we would not know what goodness is
  4. To Believe in Destiny, the good and the bad
  5. To Give Charity Despite your Love of Wealth
    • Giving to those closest to you such as relatives and friends
    • Giving to the orphans
    • Giving to the Poor
    • Giving to the travelers and competing to host them in your house
    • Giving to help someone crushed under debt, paying off student loans, or freeing a slave
  6. To Establish Salah
    • It is symbolic for all deeds we do for Allah
  7. To Give Zakah
    • It is symbolic for all deeds we do for other people
  8. To Keep Your Commitments when you have given your word
  9. To be patient during times of distress, economic hardship, and in the heat of battle.

Concluding Remarks

  • As we can see, Allah swt expects us to be engaged in our communities in order to meet the highest standard of goodness
  • Goodness is composed of moral good and religious good
  • It is a perfect balance of what it means to be goodness
  • We ask Allah swt to forgive us of our sins
  • To allow us to be agents of goodness, and to rejoin us all in Jannah.
