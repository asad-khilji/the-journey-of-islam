Surah Al Luqman

Ayah 12

  • And We definitely gave Luqman wisdom so that he might be grateful to Allah
  • And we definitely gave Luqman wisdom so that he was grateful for the sake of Allah
  • There is a time and place to speak to everyone! Luqman (A) spoke to his son when the right time came
  • Part of being a wise person is that you find something to be grateful for in every situation that you are in
  • Belief in Allah doesn’t just mean belief in the entity, the being that is Allah.
  • It leads to certain kinds of attitudes
  • The first of these attitudes is gratitude, positivity and appreciation that lead to your mentality shifting.
  • Allah says whoever is grateful is grateful for himself
  • And whoever is ungrateful then the punishment is severe
  • Allah doesn’t need people to praise Him!
  • If there was no creation, if nothing had ever been made to praise Him He would still be worthy of praise
  • It is part of His quality of being Al Hameed

Ayah 13

  • And when Luqman said to his son while he was giving him loving advice: come here young son, don’t associate anyone with Allah
  • There is a time and place to speak to everyone! Luqman (A) spoke to his son when the right time came
  • Luqman gave advice to his son in a meaningful loving and caring way
  • Luqman chose to part his advice at this time and tries to impact his son emotionally
  • Luqman calls his son in a loving way to reduce stress and feelings of harshness
  • He said come here my dear son as opposed to come here son
  • The first part of advice is to be good to Allah and to not associate any partner with Him
  • Ibn Taymiyyah categorized our slavery to Allah as such:
    1. We Pray to Him
    2. We Obey Him
    3. We Trust Him
      • This trust is unique because Allah does not have to earn it
      • We already rely on Allah on everything such as our breathing and our heart beat
      • Other people in life can and always will let you down in some way but Allah never will
    4. We Love Him
      • Your heart has to be tied to Allah
      • You cannot allow something to distract you in times of worship
      • If Allah is truly in your heart than your thought will be on Him and not on other things
      • If our love for our family, friends, and obligations is not based on our love for Allah than it can be a form of shirk
    5. Allah decides the terms of the relationship not you or me

Ayah 14

  • And We have enjoined upon man [care] for his parents.
  • His mother carried him, [increasing her] in weakness upon weakness,
  • And his weaning is in two years
  • Be grateful to Me and to your parents; to Me is the [final] destination
  • Allah (swt) mentions being grateful to Him, and then brings up our parents first
  • Because He protects us, feeds us, gives us what we need to survive, He teaches us - all of this through our parents

Ayah 15

  • But if they strive to make you take partners with Me then do not obey them but keep good company with them
  • A lot of times we feel like our parents are pushing us to something that’s bad and we can stop spending time with them
  • But Allah (swt) says we HAVE to spend more time with them in an excellent manner
  • Remain in their company decently, friendly, respectfully, and in service to them
  • There is a 99% chance that you, the child, are being entitled or arrogant, so make sure you ALWAYS act in the best way to them.
  • And follow the path of whoever turned back to Me
  • If you aren’t able to use your parents as mentors, find people who turn back to Allah
  • Find people who make you want to be better and emulate them
  • Then I will inform you of the things you used to do

Ayah 16

  • And Luqman said O my son indeed if a wrong should be the weight of a mustard seed
  • And it should be within a rock or anywhere in the heavens or in the earth
  • Allah will bring it forth
  • Indeed Allah is Subtle and Acquainted

Ayah 17

  • O my son establish the prayer and enjoin what is right
  • Forbid what is wrong and be patient over what befalls you
  • Indeed all that is of the matters requiring determination

Ayah 18

  • And do not turn your cheek in contempt toward people
  • And do not walk through the earth exultantly
  • Indeed Allah does not like everyone self-deluded and boastful

Ayah 19
