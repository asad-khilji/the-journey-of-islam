What Does Allah See In You

Multiple Plurals

    • Sunbula, Sunbulatin, Sanabil
      • Sunbula is singular
      • Sunbulatin is the weak plural
      • Sanabil is the strong plural
      • In Surah Yusuf Allah used the weak plural
      • In Surah Baqarah Allah used the strong plural
    • Nimah, Anum, Niam
      • Nimah is singular
      • Anum is the weak plural
      • Niam is the strong plural
      • Allah used the weak plural for describing how Ibrahim was grateful for only a few blessings
      • Allah uses the strong plural describing how He unleashed onto us His blessings
      • Allah used the singluar word by saying we cannot count one blessing of Allah

Saariu and Sabiqu

    • Saariu
      • Saariu means to rush
      • Allah says rush towards jannah and the forgiveness of Allah
      • Allah describes jannah as big as the seven heavens
      • Allah mentions certain kinds of people who make it to this jannah
      • Those who control their anger and have taqwa
      • This jannah is exclusive
    • Sabiqu
      • Sabiqu means to race
      • Allah says race towards jannah and the forgiveness of Allah
      • Allah describes the size of this jannah as whatever lies above
      • Allah mentions this jannah is for whoever believes in Allah and the Messenger
      • This jannah is inclusive

    The Three Words of Choice

      1. Ikhtiar
        • Iktiar means to make a purely good choice
        • It comes from the word khair
        • khair means good
        • To make a choice based on the good in something
      2. Istifa
        • Istifa means to make purely your own personal choice
        • There was no outside influence
        • No one forced you or pressured you to make a choice
        • This is how Allah chooses angels, Messengers, and Prophets
      3. Ijtiba
        • Ijtiba means to make a choice based on qualifications
        • Allah said we are chosen to be Muslim because we are qualified
        • The qualification is that we worship as Allah deserves to be worshipped
        • We cannot worship Allah as He deserves to be worshipped
        • Allah said He will make it easy for us like He made it easy for Ibrahim
        • Ibrahim went through difficult tests yet Allah made it easy for him and he passed the tests
        • Allah will make it easy for us as well
        • Our tests are not as hard as Ibrahim

      The Two Kinds of Hearts

        1. Qalb
          • Qalb means a heart that is calm
          • The changing emotions of the heart
        2. Fuad
          • Fuad means a heart that is raging with emotions
          • It also means roasting
          • A heart being roasted and intensified
          • Allah says the hearing, the seeing, and the Fuad each of them will be asked about
        • Example
          • Allah describes the heart of Musa's mother as Fuad when she threw her baby in the river
          • Then Allah describes her heart as Qalb when she is reunited with her baby
          • Allah calmed the heart of Musa's mother
