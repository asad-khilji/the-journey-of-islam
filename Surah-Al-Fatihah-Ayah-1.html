Surah Al Fatihah Ayah 1

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ

Hadith Qudsi

  • There is a Hadith Qudsi in which Allah says He has divided the prayer (Fatihah) between Allah and us
  • When we say Praise and Thanks is for Allah Master of all people, Allah says My slave has praised me
  • When we say The extremely loving and caring, Allah says My Slave has praised Me more
  • When we say Owner of the day of judgment, Allah says My Slave has glorified Me
  • When we says You alone we worship and You alone we seek help from, Allah says This is between Me and My Slave and My slave shall have whatever he or she is asking for
  • When we say Guide us to the straight path, Allah says it is guranteed
  • When we say the path of those whom You have favored, Allah says it is guaranteed
  • When we say But not the path of those who received wrath upon themselves, Allah says it is guranteed
  • When we say And not of the lost, Allah says it is guranteed

Hamd

  • Hamd means Praise and Thanks
  • You can praise without thanking and you can thank without praising
  • Musa thanks the pharoah for raising him but he does not praise him as a god
  • Ibrahim thanks his father for raising him but he does not praise his worship of the idols
  • We must have two attitudes in mind, praise and thanks
  • We cannot complain after saying this statement
  • We must find a genuine reason to praise and thank Allah
  • Our deen begins with optimism and a positive attitude
  • This is a complete change in thought and attitude
  • Hamd means genuine and sincere praise and thanks
  • Shukr means thanks but it is not genuine and sincere thanks
  • Gratitude and Thanks is a reaction
  • Hamd is praise and thanks that is not limited to a reaction

Nouns and Verbs

  • There is a difference between nouns and verbs
  • Nouns
    • It does not require anyone or anything to do it
    • It is independnent
    • It is not stuck in time
    • so it is timeless
    • It is permanent
  • Verbs
    • It requires someone or something to do it
    • It is dependent on someone or something
    • It is stuck in time
    • It is either in the past tense, present tense, or future tense
    • It is temporary
  • Hamd is a noun so it is timeless and independent from everyone and everything
  • Praise and Thanks for Allah is timeless and permanent
  • Allah does not need anyone to praise or thank Him
  • Whether we praise or thank Allah or not the praise and thanks of Allah still exists

Two kinds of sentences

  • Information Sentences or sentences whose purpose is information
  • Emotional Sentences or sentences whose purpose is emotion
  • Allah said Alhamdulillah or Praise and thanks is for Allah so it is a sentence of information and emotion
  • If Allah said Innal Alhamdillah or for sure Praise and thanks is for Allah than it would be informational sentence only

Surah Al Fatihah and Surah Al Jathiya

  • Surah Al Fatihah says Praise and Thanks is for Allah
  • Surah Al Jathiya says Praise and Thanks is only for Allah
  • Praise and thanks is only for Allah is used when arguing with people
  • Surah Al Fatihah is a statement from the heart and we already know who Allah is
  • Surah Al Fatihah is not arguing with anyone
  • In Surah Al Jathiya Allah is debating with the polytheist so Praise and Thanks is only for Allah is used
  • When saying Praise and Thanks is only for Allah, it is also saying Hamd is not for anyone else

The Name of Allah

  • When we call Allah by His name than we appreciate all the qualities of Allah
  • We can praise Allah with one word and include all His attributes by using the name Allah
  • Allah is the proper name of Allah
  • Names are very important in an introduction
  • Allah introduces Himself to us in the Fatihah so He uses His name
  • By Allah introducing to us with His name it clears any confusion of Praise or thanks being for anyone else

The Ultimate Praise and Thanks

  • Praise and Thanks could be for other people and things
  • But ultimately Praise and Thanks goes back to Allah

The Ultimate, Genuine, Sincere, & Timeless Priase and Thanks is for Allah who is independent from His creation

Rabb

  1. Malik
    • It means Master or owner
  2. Sayyed
    • The one who is in control and in charge
  3. Murrabi
    • The one who is improving us constantly
    • The one who allows us to grow and develop
  4. Qayyim

  5. Munnim
    • The one who gives us gifts over and over again

The Master and Slave Relationship

  • Allah is our Master and we are His Slaves
  • Human beings have a negative connation of slavery
  • Allah is a unique Master
  • Human beings have this delusion that they are free
  • There is no such thing as freedom
  • We are enslaved to someone or something like money, fashion, government, employers
  • When you enslave yourself to Allah than you are free from all other forms of slavery
